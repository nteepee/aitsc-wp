## Color-Coded Clarity

### White = Buckled & Occupied
Passenger sitting. Belt clicked. Everything good.

### Red = Unbuckled
Passenger sitting. Belt NOT clicked. Attention needed.

### Black = Empty Seat
No passenger. No concern. No alert.

### Yellow = Sensor Issue
Sensor fault. Maintenance needed. Still safe to drive,
but schedule a check.

---

## Why Colors Work

Drivers glance at the display 3-5 times per minute.
- Text? Takes 2 seconds to read.
- Icons? Takes 1 second to process.
- **Colors? Instant recognition.**

Red catches attention in <0.1 seconds. Evolution wired us
to notice red immediately.

**We use biology to keep passengers safe.**

## Mounting Options

### Dash Mount (Most Common)
Bracket attaches to existing dashboard surface.
- **Installation**: 2 screws or heavy-duty adhesive
- **Adjustability**: 30Â° tilt range
- **Removability**: Unbolt in 5 minutes
- **Best for**: Most vehicles

### Flush Mount (Factory Look)
Display recesses into dashboard cutout.
- **Installation**: Requires dashboard modification
- **Appearance**: Clean, integrated, OEM-style
- **Removability**: Semi-permanent
- **Best for**: New builds, premium refits

### Swing Arm (Articulated)
Display on articulated arm extends toward driver.
- **Installation**: Mounts to dash or column
- **Adjustability**: Full articulation
- **Best for**: Angled dashes, driver preference

## Color Schemes

### Standard (White/Red)
The default. Optimized for instant recognition.
White = buckled. Red = unbuckled. Black = empty.

### High Contrast
For drivers with visual impairments or bright environments.
Brighter colors. Thicker borders. Larger icons.

### Night Vision (Green)
Green display for night operations.
Preserves night vision. Common in emergency services.

### Colorblind-Friendly
Patterns + colors. Accessible to all types of color blindness.
Shapes + hues for universal recognition.
